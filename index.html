Visualizing Cattle Growth Rates via Derivatives

An interactive simulator modeling the weight gain profile of Malaysia's indigenous Kedah-Kelantan (KK) cattle using calculus.

🐄 The Bioscience Scenario

In intensive Malaysian animal husbandry, tracking the Average Daily Gain (ADG) is critical for optimizing feed efficiency and determining the precise market readiness age.

For local Kedah-Kelantan (KK) bulls, growth follows a sigmoidal (S-shaped) curve. While the weight curve $f(x)$ shows current mass, its derivative $f'(x)$ calculates the exact rate of weight gain (ADG) at any given month.

Data adapted from reference standards: "Growth Profiles of Malaysian Kedah-Kelantan Cattle" (MARDI Research Journal).

📐 The Calculus Model

Growth Function $f(x)$

$$f(x) = \frac{280}{1 + 9.36 \cdot e^{-0.15x}}$$

Yields total body weight (kg) at month $x$.
Derivative $f'(x)$ (Growth Rate)

$$f'(x) = \frac{393.12 \cdot e^{-0.15x}}{(1 + 9.36 \cdot e^{-0.15x})^2}$$

Yields instant growth rate (kg/month) at month $x$. Divide by 30 for Daily Gain.
